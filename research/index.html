How We Study Language

We use a handful of child-friendly techniques to peek inside the mind as language unfolds. Every method is safe, non-invasive, and designed to feel more like a game than an experiment.

Electroencephalography (EEG)

We record the brain's electrical activity millisecond by millisecond as participants listen to stories or sentences — no needles, no discomfort.

Adults & Children (ages 5+) ~1 hour In-person

Your brain is constantly producing tiny electrical signals — even when you're just sitting still. When you hear a word, a whole cascade of signals fires across your scalp in the first fraction of a second. EEG lets us capture that cascade in real time, giving us a window into language processing that no other technique can match.

We use a soft, stretchy cap fitted with small sensors (electrodes). The sensors sit against the scalp and pick up electrical activity through a small amount of saline gel — the same kind of gel used in a hospital for a routine ECG. Nothing is injected, nothing touches the brain, and nothing hurts.

What a visit looks like

  1. We greet you and your family in the lobby and show you around the lab.
  2. A researcher explains the cap using a teddy bear — kids can touch the gel pen and cap before anything goes on their head.
  3. We measure your child's head and select the right cap size (we have many!).
  4. Your child sits in our testing room and watches a show or movie while we place the cap and fill the electrodes with gel using a blunt gel pen — no needles, ever. Setup takes 15–25 minutes.
  5. We show a short "super scientist" video where your child can see their own brainwaves on screen.
  6. The study begins — your child listens to stories or sentences while we record. The task feels like watching TV.
  7. When we're done, the cap comes off. We give you a wipe for any gel in the hair (it rinses out easily with shampoo). Your child picks a prize from our toy box!
